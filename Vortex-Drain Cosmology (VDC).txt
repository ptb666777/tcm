Abstract

This framework proposes that observable reality is a phase-locked excitation of a deeper substrate—a non-spatial, superfluid-like medium that exists in a distinct phase from matter. The universe is finite, closed (3‑torus topology), and formed from a single eruption event that seeded the substrate with energy. Structure emerges from wave interference, gravitational collapse, and substrate dynamics, not from dark matter or dark energy.

The framework resolves the Hubble tension, explains the axis of evil, replaces dark matter with substrate viscosity, models black holes as phase-transition drains, and describes quantum behavior as the signature of the substrate interface.
1. Axioms
1.1 Substrate

    A prior-phase medium underlying observable reality.

    Not a spatial dimension; a distinct state of existence.

    Behaves as a superfluid with finite surface tension.

    Exists everywhere but is only measurable where it interfaces with matter.

1.2 Phase Transition

    Observable matter is substrate energy that has undergone a phase lock.

    Transition is one-way at cosmic scales except at black hole drains.

    The phase boundary is where quantum behavior emerges.

1.3 Topology

    The universe is a 3‑torus: finite, closed, no boundaries.

    Travel far enough in any direction returns to origin.

    Wave propagation wraps and self-interferes, creating structure.

1.4 Single Eruption

    One initial event transferred energy from substrate to observable phase.

    Not a point explosion; a uniform thin plasma with quantum noise.

    No inflation. Uniformity is built in.

1.5 Structure Formation

    Wave interference (constructive nodes) seeds matter accumulation.

    Gravitational collapse concentrates matter along interference filaments.

    Voids form where substrate thins; true voids are substrate failure zones.

1.6 Matter as Vortex

    Particles are quantized vortices in the substrate.

    Mass is substrate displacement; inertia is vortex drag.

    Stability depends on phase-lock with substrate surface tension.

1.7 Forces as Substrate Dynamics

    Gravity: low‑frequency substrate deformation (drain toward density).

    Electromagnetism: shear stress between counter‑rotating vortices.

    Strong force: surface tension holding vortex pairs.

    Weak force: vortex instability exceeding substrate tension.

1.8 Quantum Interface

    Quantum superposition: 5D filament not yet phase‑locked to 3D frame.

    Entanglement: two vortices sharing the same substrate thread.

    Wavefunction collapse: measurement forces phase‑lock.

3. Simulation Results (v12)

A 3D torus simulation (64³ grid, 500 steps) with corrected physics showed:

    Filament structure emerged from wave interference without seeding.

    Chirality was position‑dependent; opposite octants developed opposite handedness.

    Void fraction grew to >0.9, then contracted as geysers injected new matter.

    BH drain and geyser injection approached equilibrium over time (within 6% by step 400).

    Core density concentrated in late epochs (+4.7 in structure phase).

    Mixed handedness persisted across runs.

Limitations:

    Initial conditions still used uniform noise rather than true void + thin plasma.

    Gravity used peak sampling, not FFT.

    Wave‑to‑matter coupling leaked energy, causing matter growth.
